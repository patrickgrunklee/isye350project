NEXT STEPS TO CONSIDER
====================================================================================================

Option 1: Remove Days-on-Hand Constraint Entirely
  - Let the model decide optimal inventory levels
  - Only constraint: Must meet demand each period
  - Risk: May result in very low safety stock

Option 2: Use Aggregate System-Wide Days-on-Hand
  - Require total inventory across all facilities >= DoH requirement
  - Allow flexible allocation to facilities with capacity
  - Maintains safety stock but allows redistribution

Option 3: Accept Partial Infeasibility
  - Run full multiperiod optimization (not just slack analysis)
  - Allow expansion at Sacramento and Austin
  - Maximize use of Columbus current capacity
  - Document that Columbus needs external warehousing or product line reduction

Option 4: Analyze Which SKUs are Driving Columbus Bottleneck
  - Identify top 3-5 SKUs consuming most Columbus pallet space
  - Consider product-specific strategies:
    - Move specific product lines to Sacramento/Austin entirely
    - Reduce days-on-hand for specific bulky products
    - Outsource storage for specific SKUs
