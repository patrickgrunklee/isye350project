MODEL SPECIFICATIONS
====================================================================================================

Key Features:
1. Pure-SKU Continuous Packing
   - Shelves with only ONE SKU type: Use continuous volume-based packing
   - Mixed-SKU shelves: Follow discrete 7-item structure
   - Dramatically increases capacity per shelf (up to 20-100× improvement)

2. Columbus Hard Constraint
   - Columbus CANNOT expand (per project requirements)
   - Columbus MUST operate at 99%+ pallet utilization if Sac/Austin need expansion
   - Forces maximum use of existing Columbus capacity before expanding elsewhere

3. Days-on-Hand Safety Stock
   - Traditional safety stock calculation: (daily demand) × (DoH days)
   - Three scenarios tested: 10/3, 5/2, and 3/1 business days

KEY INSIGHTS FROM MODEL EVOLUTION
====================================================================================================

1. 3D Bin Packing → Pure-SKU Continuous Packing
   Before: Columbus needed 22,580 more shelves (735% over capacity)
   After:  Columbus fits within 3,080 existing shelves
   Improvement: 100% - completely eliminated Columbus expansion need

2. Days-on-Hand Impact
   Reducing DoH from 10/3 to 3/1 saved 698 shelves (10% reduction)
   Lower safety stock = less inventory = fewer shelves needed

3. Columbus Utilization Constraint
   Without constraint: Columbus might be underutilized while Sac/Austin expand
   With constraint: Columbus at 100% before any other facility expands
   Result: Maximizes use of existing assets, minimizes total expansion cost

4. Pure-SKU Configuration Examples
   - SKUC1 (chairs): 20 units/shelf vs 14 (43% improvement)
   - SKUD3 (desks): 22 units/shelf vs 7 (214% improvement)
   - SKUT2 (textbooks): 112+ units/shelf vs 7 (1,500%+ improvement)
